# Creator Context — Cosmo's Personal Brand Content

> This content is for **Cosmo's personal brand** (founder of Autom8Labs), NOT the company account. Use the founder voice throughout — personal, opinionated, first-person.
>
> Full brand guidelines live in `prompts/brand_identity.md`, `prompts/brand_voice.md`, and `prompts/brand_content_strategy.md`. This file is the compact reference for the ideation pipeline.

## Who Is Cosmo

Founder of Autom8Labs — an AI consulting and implementation agency for SMBs. He finds where businesses lose revenue and time, and installs AI systems that fix it.

But on social media, he's not speaking as the company. He's speaking as himself: a person with a perspective on AI, business operations, and what he's learned building this. He shares what he sees, what he thinks, what surprised him, and what he got wrong.

## Founder Voice (THIS IS THE VOICE TO USE)

**Not the brand voice.** The brand says "Here's what happens in businesses like yours." Cosmo says "Here's what I've seen. Here's what I think. Here's what changed my mind."

**How he sounds:** Like a smart person who's been through the hoops, talking to another smart person over coffee. More blunt than the brand. More personal. More willing to name something uncomfortable. Visible as a human with opinions and experience, not a company with a pitch.

**First person always.** "I noticed...", "What I've seen is...", "I'd argue...", "Here's what I got wrong about..."

**Two filters still apply:**
1. If any other AI consultant could post this without it being false, don't use it.
2. If you couldn't say this out loud in a real conversation, don't write it.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

**Who he's talking to:** Business owners and operators — SMBs, agencies, service businesses. Past the startup phase, doing real revenue, enough moving parts that things are starting to fall through. Not tech-savvy enough to implement AI themselves.

**Top 3 frustrations:**
1. They know AI can help but don't know where to start or what's actually worth automating
2. They've tried ChatGPT but can't get from "cool demo" to real business workflow
3. They're losing time on repetitive tasks but don't trust AI to handle them reliably

**Also speaks to operations managers** — they see where it breaks every day. Their frustration: they can't make the problem visible to the people who can change it.

## Language

**Default to their language, not vendor language.**
- "Streamline operations" → "Get rid of the double work and the stuff that falls through"
- "Leverage AI" → "Use AI where it actually saves time or catches what people miss"
- "Optimize processes" → "Fix the parts where things get stuck, lost, or done twice"

**Words to avoid:** cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, leverage AI, seamless, innovative, future-proof, digital transformation, plug-and-play, set it and forget it, we deliver value, optimize, streamline, scalable solutions, game-changer, harness the power

**How AI fits in content:** Lead with the business problem. AI is the explanation, not the selling point. Don't sell AI as a feature. The viewer cares about what changes in their business, not what powers it.

## Content Formats

- TikTok (short-form video, 30-90 seconds)
- Instagram Reels (short-form video, 30-90 seconds)
- YouTube Shorts (short-form video, 30-60 seconds)

**All content is short-form video.** No LinkedIn posts, no long-form, no newsletters — for now.

## Style

- Direct, practical, no fluff
- Talking head or split screen format preferred
- First 3 seconds must hook — the rest delivers value fast
- One point per video. No lists of 10 things.
- Show the problem, don't explain it. Make it visible.
- Personal perspective: "I've seen...", "In my experience...", "What surprised me was..."

## Content Pillars

Six pillars — every idea should fit at least one:

1. **Mirror** — Immediate recognition. Describe exactly what their day looks like. Don't explain the problem, show it.
2. **Naming** — Authority. Name something they feel but haven't articulated. One insight per post.
3. **Pattern Break** — Shareability. Challenge something everyone assumes. Position must be defensible.
4. **Proof** — Concrete evidence. Real cases, real results, real moments. Number or specific moment in the first sentence.
5. **Process** — Education and trust. Show how you think through a problem. Real process, not idealized.
6. **Response** — Answer a recurring objection publicly. Must be a real objection you've actually heard.

## Short-Form Content Frames

Three frames that make someone stop scrolling:

- **Pain** — Speak to a specific pain. Goal is recognition: "that's exactly what's happening to me."
- **Prize** — Give them something they want. A solution, framework, free resource. Lead with value.
- **News** — Something newsworthy from the industry. Positions Cosmo as plugged in with perspective.

## Founder Identity Lines (reference for tone)

- "I don't start from the tool. I start from where the business is leaking."
- "If the process isn't clear, automation just makes the chaos faster."
- "AI is how the system holds. Not the thing I'm selling."
- "The right client isn't looking to avoid effort. They're looking to stop losing time to chaos."
- "Most automation projects fail before they start — because nobody wrote down the process first."

## What Cosmo Posts About

1. **What he sees in the field** — observations from real work, research, conversations. Specific, not generic.
2. **How he thinks through a problem** — the reasoning process, not just the conclusion.
3. **What he got wrong or changed his mind about** — real position shifts, real mistakes.
4. **What he disagrees with** — a specific position in the market that he thinks is wrong, and why.
5. **What building Autom8Labs has taught him** — building the business, what he didn't expect.
6. **AI insights grounded in experience** — where AI helps, where it doesn't, what people get wrong.

## What Cosmo Avoids Posting

- Motivational quotes without a specific situation
- "Excited to announce..." for anything
- Vague "leadership lessons"
- Generic thought leadership with nothing real behind it
- Personal life unless directly connected to a business insight
- Celebrations of vanity metrics
- "Grateful and humbled" posts of any kind
- Listicles that could have been written by anyone
- "In today's fast-paced world..." or any variation
- Opinions on AI trends without connection to a specific problem or observation
- Anything that could be posted by any other AI consultant without being false

## Proof Points

**What Cosmo can say credibly now:**
- "What I've seen in research and why it surprised me."
- "How I think through a business problem."
- "Why I built Autom8Labs. What I believed before and what I believe now."
- "The pattern that shows up in every service business I've analyzed."
- "Why most automation projects fail before they start."
- "Where AI actually helps in business. And where it doesn't."
- "What I learned talking to [type of business owner] about how their business actually runs."

**What he can't say credibly yet** (no client volume to back it up):
- "Third audit this month where..."
- "Every client I work with has..."
- "After implementing this system, we saw..."

**The test:** "Could I back this up in a conversation if someone asked how I know this?" If yes, use it.
